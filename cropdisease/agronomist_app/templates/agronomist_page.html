Tomato Late Blight Management
Disease Alert Tomatoes High Priority
Dr. James Mwangi
Plant Pathologist • Last updated: April 15, 2023

Overview

Tomato late blight, caused by the pathogen Phytophthora infestans, is one of the most devastating diseases of tomatoes worldwide. It can cause complete crop loss under favorable conditions.

Symptoms

  • Irregular, water-soaked lesions on leaves that rapidly expand
  • White fungal growth on underside of leaves during humid conditions
  • Dark, firm lesions on stems and petioles
  • Brown, sunken lesions on fruit that may become covered with white fungal growth
Conditions Favoring Disease
  • Cool temperatures (15-21°C)
  • High humidity (>90%)
  • Extended leaf wetness periods
  • Poor air circulation
Prevention
  • Plant resistant varieties when available
  • Ensure proper spacing for air circulation
  • Avoid overhead irrigation
  • Apply preventive fungicides in high-risk periods

Management Strategies

Cultural Control

Implement crop rotation with non-solanaceous crops for 3-4 years. Remove and destroy infected plant material. Use drip irrigation instead of overhead watering.

Chemical Control

When disease is detected or conditions are favorable, apply fungicides. Recommended products include:

Product Active Ingredient Application Interval
Bordeaux Mixture Copper sulfate + lime 7-10 days
Revus Mandipropamid 10-14 days
Ridomil Gold Metalaxyl-M + copper 14 days
Biological Control

Some biological options show promise in early trials:

  • Bacillus subtilis strains
  • Trichoderma harzianum
  • Pseudomonas fluorescens

Monitoring and Early Detection

Regular field inspections are critical. Use the Agricure mobile app to report suspected cases and receive localized alerts when disease risk is high in your area.

Conditions favorable for disease spread. High humidity and recent rainfall increase risk.
Field Observations

Irregular brown lesions on leaves with white fungal growth on undersides during humid conditions. Lesions initially appear on older leaves and spread upward. Some fruit showing dark, sunken lesions. Disease appears to be spreading from the northeast corner of the field where drainage is poor.

Symptoms Checklist
Recommended Actions
Urgent Action Required: This disease can spread rapidly and cause complete crop loss if not treated immediately.
Immediate Actions
  1. Remove and destroy severely infected plants (burn or bury deep)
  2. Apply copper-based fungicide (e.g., Bordeaux mixture) to all plants
  3. Improve air circulation by pruning lower leaves
  4. Avoid overhead irrigation
Long-term Prevention
  • Rotate crops with non-solanaceous plants for 3-4 years
  • Plant resistant varieties next season (e.g., Mountain Magic, Defiant)
  • Improve field drainage in affected areas
  • Apply mulch to prevent soil splash
